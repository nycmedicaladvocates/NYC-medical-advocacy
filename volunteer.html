What volunteers do

  • Help people find the right place to start
  • Help prepare questions before calls or visits
  • Help organize forms, letters, and paperwork
  • Connect people to trusted public resources

Who we need

  • Reliable volunteers who communicate clearly
  • People comfortable with phone, text, and online forms
  • Students interested in service, medicine, or public health
  • People who can follow boundaries and use trusted sources

How it works

Simple, practical, flexible
  • Flexible volunteering based on availability
  • Remote-friendly for many tasks
  • Simple scripts and guidance provided
  • Focus on navigation support, not clinical advice

Volunteer standards

  • Use official or reputable sources whenever possible
  • Respect privacy and boundaries
  • Do not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations
  • Escalate emergencies to 911 and mental health crises to 988

Why volunteer

Meaningful community impact

Many people get stuck trying to figure out where to start, who to call, and what paperwork to prepare. Volunteers help reduce confusion and help people move forward.

Important boundary

Navigation support only

NYC Medical Advocates provides free healthcare navigation support. We do not provide medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or emergency services.
