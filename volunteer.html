Volunteer

Help New Yorkers navigate healthcare

Volunteers provide practical support: helping people find where to start, preparing questions, organizing documents, and connecting them to trusted public and nonprofit resources.

100% free healthcare navigation support • Not medical advice • Not for emergencies
About the program See impact Privacy and boundaries

What volunteers do

Practical, non-clinical support
  • Clarify the person’s goal and identify the next step
  • Find official and reputable resources
  • Help prepare a short call script or question list
  • Help organize documents and timelines
  • Support follow-up when appropriate

What volunteers need

Core expectations
  • Clear communication and reliability
  • Respect for privacy and boundaries
  • Comfort using forms, websites, and public resources
  • A commitment to not guessing when unsure

Before you help

  • Read the safety boundaries first
  • Use official sources whenever possible
  • Ask for review when a situation is unclear

During a case

  • Clarify the goal
  • Identify the next concrete step
  • Document what resource was shared

Escalate immediately

  • Immediate danger or severe symptoms → 911
  • Mental health crisis → 988
  • Requests for medical advice → licensed clinician

Volunteer standards

Scope and boundaries

Volunteers do not provide medical advice, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, or emergency response. Volunteers should protect privacy, keep notes factual and brief, and stay within the program’s navigation role.

Read more about boundaries
Use official or reputable sources whenever possible. Do not collect more information than needed. Escalate urgent safety concerns to emergency services or crisis resources. When something is unclear, pause and review before responding.
