FAQ

Frequently asked questions

These are common questions about what NYC Medical Advocates does, who the site is for, and how to use the program safely.

100% free healthcare navigation support • Not medical advice • Not for emergencies
What does NYC Medical Advocates do?
We help people figure out where to start, who to call, what to ask, and what documents to prepare. We focus on healthcare navigation, not medical care.
Do you give medical advice?
No. We do not provide medical advice, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, or emergency services.
Who is this for?
The site is for people in NYC who are uninsured, confused about bills or paperwork, trying to find care, supporting a family member, or looking for trusted public resources.
What should I do if it is urgent?
If there is immediate danger or a medical emergency, call 911. If there is a mental health crisis, call or text 988.
What is the fastest way to reach you?
Calling or texting is usually the fastest option for non-urgent requests.
Is the online form monitored in real time?
No. The online request form is not monitored in real time.
Can you help if I do not speak English?
Yes. We have Spanish-language pages, and many city services and hospitals can provide interpreters.
Do you decide whether I qualify for programs like NYC Care?
No. Eligibility is determined by the official program, not by us. We can only help you find the official starting point and prepare questions.
