WHAT WE HELP WITH

Services we provide
  • Finding clinics, hospitals, or the right place to start
  • Low-cost and uninsured care options
  • Medical bills, denials, and confusing letters
  • Insurance questions and public coverage starting points
  • Medication cost questions and trusted assistance resources
  • Preparing for appointments and follow-up calls
  • Transportation and language access questions
  • Mental health and community resource navigation

IMPORTANT LIMITS

What we do not do
  • We do not provide medical advice
  • We do not diagnose or recommend treatment
  • We do not replace doctors, nurses, or emergency services
  • We do not guarantee eligibility, appointments, coverage, or outcomes

Our role is to help you understand your options and identify the next practical step.

HOW IT WORKS

Simple support, one step at a time
  1. You contact us by call, text, or online form.
  2. We review what you need help with.
  3. We help identify the next step and share trusted resources.
  4. When appropriate, we help you prepare questions, documents, or a call plan.

WHO THIS IS FOR

Common reasons people reach out
  • You are uninsured and do not know where to begin
  • You got a bill or letter you do not understand
  • You need help figuring out what office to call
  • You want help getting organized before an appointment
  • You are helping a family member or patient navigate the system

WAYS TO REACH US

Pick the easiest option
  • Call: 201-822-5428
  • Text: 201-822-5428
  • Online request form: scroll down and submit

Call or text is usually best if you want the fastest response.

WHAT TO PREPARE

Helpful information to have ready
  • A short summary of what you need help with
  • Your best callback number and times
  • If bills: the bill, account number, and date of service
  • If insurance: your card and any letters you received
  • If appointments: the clinic name, date, and any instructions

ONLINE REQUEST FORM

Submit a non-urgent request

This form is not monitored in real time. If you are in immediate danger or have severe symptoms, call 911. If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, call or text 988.

If the form does not load, call or text 201-822-5428.

LANGUAGE ACCESS

Spanish and other languages

We have a Spanish page, and many hospitals, clinics, and city services can provide interpreters. For many city services, you can also call 311 and ask for help in your language.
