NYC Medical Advocates is a free volunteer program that helps people navigate healthcare in New York City. We help people figure out where to start, who to call, what to ask, and what documents to prepare. We focus on practical next steps using trusted public and nonprofit resources.

WHO WE ARE

A free volunteer navigation program

We are not a clinic, hospital, insurance company, or emergency service. We are a free community-based program that helps people understand options and take the next practical step.

We use official or highly reputable sources whenever possible, including city agencies, hospitals, public programs, and nonprofit organizations.

WHAT WE DO

Practical help when the system feels confusing
  • Help identify where to start
  • Help find the right office, clinic, or resource
  • Help prepare questions before a call or appointment
  • Help organize bills, letters, and paperwork
  • Help connect people to trusted public resources

WHO THIS IS FOR

Common reasons people come to us
  • You are uninsured and not sure where to begin
  • You got a bill or letter you do not understand
  • You need help figuring out what office to call
  • You want help getting organized before an appointment
  • You are helping a family member or patient navigate the system
  • You want help finding trusted resources in one place

HOW IT WORKS

One clear step at a time
  1. Start here and review the options.
  2. Browse the directory or contact us by call, text, or form.
  3. We help identify the next step and trusted resources.
  4. When appropriate, we help you prepare questions, documents, or a call plan.

CALL OR TEXT US

BEFORE YOU REACH OUT

Helpful information to have ready
  • A short summary of what you need help with
  • Your best callback number and times
  • If bills: the bill, account number, and date of service
  • If insurance: your card and any letters you received
  • If appointments: the clinic name, date, and any instructions

ONLINE REQUEST FORM

LANGUAGE ACCESS

Spanish and other languages

We have a Spanish page, and many hospitals, clinics, and city services can provide interpreters. For many city services, you can also call 311 and ask for help in your language.
