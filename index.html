Free healthcare navigation help in NYC

Clear next steps when healthcare feels overwhelming.

We help New Yorkers find care, understand their options, and connect to trusted free and low-cost health-related resources. We support people dealing with appointments, portals, bills, paperwork, transportation, medication costs, and confusing next steps.

100% free support • Navigation help only • Not medical advice • Not for emergencies

Emergency

Call 911

Immediate danger, severe symptoms, overdose, chest pain, trouble breathing, or any other medical emergency.

Mental health crisis

Call or text 988

24/7 support for mental health crisis, emotional distress, or suicidal thoughts.

Non-urgent help

Call or text 201-822-5428

For free navigation help with care, appointments, bills, portals, forms, and trusted resources.

Who we help

For any New Yorker who needs a clearer place to start

  • People who are uninsured or worried about cost
  • Patients and families confused by bills, letters, or paperwork
  • Seniors, caregivers, and people who need help with appointments or forms
  • People trying to use MyChart or another patient portal
  • People looking for recovery, naloxone, or other harm reduction resources
  • People experiencing homelessness or unstable housing who need help finding care

What we do

We provide practical navigation support. We do not diagnose, recommend treatment, prescribe medications, replace your doctor, or provide emergency services.

How it works

One practical step at a time

  1. Start with Start Here, Find Care, or the Directory.
  2. Call, text, or submit the non-urgent form.
  3. We help identify the next step and point you to trusted resources.
  4. For emergencies, call 911. For mental health crisis, call or text 988.

We aim to make healthcare navigation less confusing, especially for people facing cost, language, technology, housing, or paperwork barriers.
